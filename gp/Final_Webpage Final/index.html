MoMA Data Visualization

The Museum of Modern Art (MoMa) was established in 1929, and today contains over 200,000 pieces of work spanning roughly 150 years. Through exploration of the data made available from MoMa, it is clear there is a disparity between gender representation and the artworks found in the collection.

A multifaceted approach was utilized in investigating gender representation in the MoMa collection. Using gender, comparisons were drawn between overall artwork acquisition; artist nationality; acquired artwork classification; most productive age ranges; and age of artist recognition.

Male and Female Artists Distribution Across the World

Of all artists whose artworks were acquired by MoMa, there are much more males than females. Besides, geologically, most artists were from the US and Europe.

Artwork Classification Acquisition by Gender

The top five classifications of artworks in MoMa are photography, print, drawing, painting, and sculpture. The total acquisition of these types of artwork have steadily increased since the 1940s, but at what rate according to gender?

Although MoMa began collecting artwork in 1929, it was not until the 1970s when female artist works began to be acquired in larger numbers. In the early 2000s, female artists began to equalize a little in comparison to their male counterparts.

Although most contributions have been from male artists, there are a few categories and years where female artists had a significant amount of work acquired. In 2012, female artists had roughly 30% more print artworks. In the later 2000s, male and female artists who created sculptures and paintings were acquired at a more equal amount.

At what age was an artwork created?

Majority of artworks are produced when an artist was around 30-50 ages regardless of genders. And it decreases as male artists getting older. However, artworks made by female overall create more artworks when they get older which means female artists tend to be more active around 30-40 ages and around 80-90. From another perspective, it may show that female artists overall has more obstacles and less freedom in terms of art creating.
